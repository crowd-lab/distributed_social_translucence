You are being asked to be in a research study that seeks to understand the impact of being observed while doing work. You have just completed the first part of this IRB-approved study. To gather data that was as natural as possible, it was important that you did not know you were participating in the study while doing so. We are not building a new social networking website. We are also not interested in the moderation process, the focus of our study is the impact of observation on the moderation task. This is known as a ‘deception study’, and the entire study protocol (including the deception) has undergone IRB review at approval.

Your participation up to now involved the task you just completed. We would also like you to complete a demographic survey, and we do not expect to take up more than 20 minutes of your time (including the task(s) you have already completed). We would like to use the data we just collected as a part of a research study and the next step is a demographic survey, should you consent.

Your alternative is to not participate in this study. If we may include your data in this study, please click ‘I consent’, and you will be redirected to the survey. If not, please click ‘I DO NOT consent’.

Your HIT will be approved and we will compensate you regardless of your decision to consent or not.

Possible risks associated with this research include the additional stress of being observed while completing a moderation task (which you have already completed).

You may not receive a direct benefit if you agree to participate. However, people in the future may benefit from the information obtained from this research.
